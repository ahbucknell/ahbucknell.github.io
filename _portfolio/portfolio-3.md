Wheat blast is a devastating fungal disease which attacks wheat alongside other grass species. Currently, the causal agent of wheat blast, *Magnaporthe oryzae* Triticum, is not found within the UK and is limited to the warmer climates of S. America and Africa. However, following my 3rd year literature review of possible bio-terror threats to the UK agronomy, wheat blast was highlighted of great concern.

This idea was taken forward into my 3rd year research proposal which focused on the plant defence initiator (PDI) Isotianil to combat wheat blast infections. Isotianil is currently used to combat rice blast, a fungal disease attacking rice which is caused by a different pathovar of the same species, *M. oryzae* Oryzae. While isotianil is not currently used to combat wheat blast, [Portz *et al.* (2021)](https://doi.org/10.1007/s41348-020-00378-y) showed that it is effective when applied to wheat seedlings. This project proposal aimed to conduct a cost-benefit analysis between single or double isotianil treatments to determine whether the additional treatment costs brought higher levels of protection.

Both the project proposal and literature review were supervised by [Professor Jeremy Pritchard](https://www.birmingham.ac.uk/schools/biosciences/staff/profile.aspx?ReferenceId=9728&Name=professor-jeremy-pritchard).

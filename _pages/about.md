Hey! I'm an  incoming PhD student at [The Sainsbury Laboratory](https://www.tsl.ac.uk/) in Norwich, UK. My research focuses on fungal effector biology within _Magnaporthe oryzae_, a phytopathogenic fungus affecting rice and wheat.

Previously, I completed my MSci Biological Sciences degree at the University of Birmingham. It was during my studies here I developed an interest in transposon biology, initially in plants but later in fungi. This led me to undertake summer research positions both at Birmingham but also abroad at the Gregor Mendel Institute in Vienna, Austria. During my final year I completed my Master's research project on a novel group of fungal transposons known as _Starships_, which served as my introduction into host-pathogen interactions.

As I start my PhD research I hope to develop a better understanding of fungal-plant pathology, though am always looking for a way to incorporate my passion for transposon biology. I aim to make an impact in agricultural biosecurity through bringing the concepts of transposon biology and fungal crop disease more into the public domain through related impacts.

I completed my undergraduate studies at the University of Birmingham where I was awarded a 1:1 in my MSci Biological Sciences degree. It was during my time at Birmingham where I developed an interest in transposon biology, initially in plants but then in fungi. This led me to undertaking a Master’s research project on a novel group of transposons known as Starships.

As I start my PhD I hope to develop a better understanding of fungal plant pathology, as well as research into possible associations between effectors and transposons. I aim to make an impact in agricultural biosecurity through this and aim to bring the concepts of transposon biology and fungal crop diseases more into the public domain through its related impacts.

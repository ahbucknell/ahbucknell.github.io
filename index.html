About me

Decoding the arsenal of
fungal invaders

I'm a PhD candidate at The Sainsbury Laboratory (TSL), one of the world's leading plant science institutes in Norwich, UK. Supervised by Prof. Nick Talbot, my work focuses on the molecular biology of the blast fungus Magnaporthe oryzae — one of the most destructive crop pathogens on the planet.

To infect plants, M. oryzae secretes effector proteins that suppress immunity and manipulate host biology. I use novel protein design and computational structural methods to probe how these effectors function — developing tools to answer questions that are difficult to address through conventional fungal genetics.

Alongside computational and bench work, I care about science communication and making complex biology accessible — from public talks at Pint of Science to open-data workflows.

Current research

What I'm working on now

01
Novel protein design for effector investigation
Developing protein design tools to directly probe the molecular function of M. oryzae effectors — bypassing the limitations of classical fungal genetics to access previously intractable biological questions.
02
Structural characterisation of fungal effectors
Applying AlphaFold2-Multimer, molecular docking, and evolutionary conservation analysis to model the three-dimensional architecture of M. oryzae effectors and identify functional sites.
03
Integrated structural bioinformatics pipelines
Building computational workflows that combine structure prediction, surface chemistry, and conservation data to prioritise experimentally testable hypotheses for wet-lab follow-up.
